Group: 2nd Grade Date: February 10th
Theme/Topic: Expressing Daily Routines  Duration:  45 minutes
Standards (National: Guide 22) – School: National Standards: Speaking Skills:
Encourages students to speak in complete sentences, use appropriate vocabulary, and organize ideas logically.
Facilitates oral interaction in collaborative and social contexts.                                                                                  School Standards Oral Expression Goals:
Supports the development of students' oral presentation and public speaking skills.
Provides students with opportunities to apply language skills in meaningful and practical ways.
Learning Objective: By end of lesson students will… Students will describe their daily routines using simple present tense.
(Measurable & aligned to learning plan and activities.)
Problem Anticipation Problem: Studens coming late Solutions (Plan B, C):
Plan B: Start with a brief stretching exercise or energizer game (e.g., "Simon Says" using action words) to refocus energy and engagement.
Plan C: If latecomers disrupt the warm-up, assign a student helper to quickly brief them while others continue.
Vocabulary: Materials Required:
Actions: wake up, brush teeth, take a shower, eat breakfast, go to school, study, play, do homework, watch TV, go to bed.
Time Words: in the morning, in the afternoon, in the evening, at night, every day.
Questions and Responses:
What time do you...?
I wake up at 7 a.m.		 Flashcards with images of daily routine activities
A whiteboard or large poster for writing vocabulary and examples.
Dry-erase markers or chalk for writing on the board.
Printable worksheets with daily routine illustrations for optional practice.
 Presentation/warm up:
Introduction/Background Information (Why are we learning this, what does my student already know, what can we look forward to learning about in this lesson?)
Warm-up (Pre):

Activity: Teacher shows flashcards of daily routines and acts out a few. Students guess the action.
Objective: Activate prior knowledge and introduce the topic.
Presentation (Pre):

Activity: The teacher introduces vocabulary for daily routines with flashcards and examples (e.g., "I wake up at 7 a.m.").
Objective: Teach key vocabulary and phrases.
Instructions (Step-by-step details of how the lesson will be undertaken)
Warm-up:

"Look at the flashcards. What is this person doing? Raise your hand to answer."
"Now, let’s act out these routines. If I show the card for brushing teeth, pretend to brush your teeth!"
Presentation:

"I’ll say each action and show the card. Repeat after me: wake up, brush teeth..."
"Now let’s talk about time. For example, I wake up at 7 a.m.. What time do you wake up?"
Practice:

"Work with a partner. Ask them about their daily routines. Use this question: What time do you... ?"
"Write down your partner's answers, and be ready to share!"
Production:

"In your groups, create a short dialogue about your daily routines. Practice speaking clearly."
"Now, each group will present their dialogue to the class."
Assessment:

"Listen carefully as your classmates present. You’ll help me check if they use the right words!"
Practice:  Teacher models the activity  Teacher - student interaction
Activity: In pairs, students practice speaking by asking each other about their daily routines (e.g., "What time do you wake up?").
Objective: Build confidence and fluency through guided interaction.
Production: 
Production (Post):

Activity: Students form small groups and create a short dialogue about daily routines, presenting it to the class.
Objective: Encourage spontaneous use of language.
Assessment (Post):

Activity: Teacher uses a checklist to assess vocabulary usage, sentence structure, and clarity during group presentations.

Lesson Plan Student's name: Carmen Katheryne Tumay Pulido
Group: 3rd Grade Date: February 12th
Theme/Topic: Shopping Role-Play Duration:  45 minutes
Standards (National: Guide 22) – School: National Standards: Speaking Skills:
Encourages students to speak in complete sentences, use appropriate vocabulary, and organize ideas logically.
Facilitates oral interaction in collaborative and social contexts.                                                                                  School Standards Oral Expression Goals:
Supports the development of students' oral presentation and public speaking skills.
Provides students with opportunities to apply language skills in meaningful and practical ways.
Learning Objective: By end of lesson students will… Students will role-play a shopping scenario using vocabulary for items, prices, and polite requests.
(Measurable & aligned to learning plan and activities.)
Problem Anticipation Possible Situation: Extended assembly time prior to class or behavioral issues Solutions (Plan B, C):
Plan B: Simplify the activity by having students describe items without using props (e.g., "I’d like two bananas, please").
Plan C: If time is limited, focus on pair practice instead of full-group presentations to ensure participation within the available window.
Vocabulary: Materials Required:
Items (Groceries): apple, banana, bread, milk, eggs, rice, chocolate, juice, cheese, soap.
Prices and Numbers: dollar, cents, how much, $1.50, $3.20.
Polite Phrases:
Can I have...?
How much is this?
Thank you!
You’re welcome!		 Play money (or printed mock currency).
Picture cards of grocery items with labeled prices.
Price tags for role-play items.
Printable dialogue samples or scripts for guided practice.
A basket or bag to mimic shopping in role-play scenarios.
 Presentation/warm up:
Introduction/Background Information (Why are we learning this, what does my student already know, what can we look forward to learning about in this lesson?)
Warm-up (Pre):

Activity: Quick game: Teacher describes an item (e.g., "It’s red, round, and you can eat it"), and students guess what it is.
Objective: Engage students and review vocabulary for shopping items.
Presentation (Pre):

Activity: Teacher demonstrates a shopping dialogue (e.g., "Can I have 2 apples, please?"). Vocabulary and polite phrases are highlighted.
Objective: Teach key phrases and sentence patterns.
Instructions (Step-by-step details of how the lesson will be undertaken)
Practice:

"Pair up with your partner. One of you will be the shopper, and the other will be the seller. Use this dialogue: Can I have...? How much is it?"
"Take turns being the shopper and the seller. Practice speaking clearly and politely."
Production:

"Now, in groups of three, create your own shopping scene. Use the items and prices on the cards."
"Be creative! You can add new items or funny situations. You’ll act it out for the class."
Assessment:

"While your classmates present, listen for polite phrases like thank you and you’re welcome. After each presentation, we’ll share what we liked about it."
Practice: Teacher models the activity  Teacher - student interaction
Practice (While):

Activity: Students pair up to practice a shopping dialogue, taking turns being the shopper and the seller.
Objective: Provide structured speaking practice.
Production: 
Production (Post):

Activity: Groups of three create a mini role-play scene in a "market." Each group presents their scene to the class.
Objective: Encourage creative use of language in a real-world context.
Assessment (Post):

Activity: Teacher assesses dialogues based on fluency, accuracy, and use of polite expressions using a rubric.
                    
INTEGRAL PEDAGOGICAL PRACTICE - 518018
Lesson Plan Student's name: Carmen Katheryne Tumay Pulido
Group: 4thd Grade Date: February 14th
Theme/Topic: Telling a Story with Pictures Duration:  45 minutes
Standards (National: Guide 22) – School: National Standards: Speaking Skills:
Encourages students to speak in complete sentences, use appropriate vocabulary, and organize ideas logically.
Facilitates oral interaction in collaborative and social contexts.                                                                                  School Standards Oral Expression Goals:
Supports the development of students' oral presentation and public speaking skills.
Provides students with opportunities to apply language skills in meaningful and practical ways.
Learning Objective: By end of lesson students will… Students will create and orally present a short story using a sequence of pictures.
Materials: Picture cards showing sequential events, a story prompt (e.g., “A Day at the Beach”).
(Measurable & aligned to learning plan and activities.)
Problem Anticipation Possible situatio: students are distracted after recess or technical difficulties with visual aids. Solutions:  (Plan B, C):
Plan B: Use a single set of picture cards and conduct the activity as a whole-class brainstorming session instead of pair work.
Plan C: Allow students to create their own story sequences by drawing simple stick figures or improvising a story without visuals.
Vocabulary: Materials Required:
Sequence Words: first, then, next, after that, finally.
Descriptive Actions: walk, run, jump, laugh, play, talk, sleep, eat, cry, smile.
Places and Objects: park, beach, house, school, ball, kite, tree, sun, cloud, sand.
Basic Sentence Starters:
Once upon a time…
There was…
One day…		 Picture cards depicting sequential events for storytelling (e.g., a day at the park, visiting a zoo).
A small board or chart to demonstrate how to arrange the pictures in order.
Sentence starters or linking word prompts on cards (e.g., "Once upon a time," "Then," "Finally").
Coloring materials (optional) for students to create their own visual storyboards.
 Presentation/warm up:
Introduction/Background Information (Why are we learning this, what does my student already know, what can we look forward to learning about in this lesson?)
Warm-up (Pre):

Activity: Teacher shows a random picture and asks students to guess what could be happening in it.
Objective: Activate imagination and creativity.
Presentation (Pre):

Activity: Teacher models a short story using 3-4 picture cards, explaining how to connect ideas using simple connectors (e.g., "first," "then," "finally").
Objective: Teach narrative structure and linking words.
Instructions (Step-by-step details of how the lesson will be undertaken)
Warm-up:

"Look at this picture. What do you see? Who is in the picture? What might they be doing?"
"Let’s make some guesses together about what could happen next."
Presentation:

"I’ll show you three pictures in a sequence. Listen as I tell a short story about them: First, the boy wakes up. Then, he goes to the park. Finally, he plays soccer."
"Now, repeat the story after me. Let’s practice using first, then, next, and finally."
Practice:

"Work with a partner. Look at the pictures I’ve given you. Arrange them in the right order and think about what happens in each one."
"Practice telling a short story using the pictures. Take turns telling the story to each other."
Production:

"Now, with your partner, tell your story to the class. Speak loudly and clearly. Don’t forget to use first, then, next, and finally!"
Assessment:

"As we listen to each story, we’ll check: Did the story make sense? Did they use sequence words? After each group, let’s give them one compliment!"
Practice: Teacher models the activity  Teacher - student interaction
Practice (While):

Activity: Students work in pairs to arrange picture cards into a logical sequence and brainstorm a short story.
Objective: Practice narrative planning and speaking in a low-pressure setting.
Production: 
Production (Post):

Activity: Each pair presents their story to the class, describing the events in order.
Objective: Develop confidence in public speaking and storytelling.
Assessment (Post):

Activity: Teacher evaluates each presentation for creativity, coherence, and use of linking words.
